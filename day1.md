# 2. Day 1

## 2.1. The Ascent Begins

**09:35** — The conversation with the two hikers lasted no more than five minutes. The first guy mentioned they had spent the night higher up and were now descending because the ridge conditions were rapidly deteriorating with heavy snow accumulation. They looked exhausted, their gear dusted with frost. I thanked them, wished them a safe descent, and adjusted the straps of my backpack. 

> **[Confession]:** This was the first physical warning the mountains handed to me. Two equipped hikers were actively retreating from the exact direction I was heading. A rational solo hiker would have stopped right there to re-evaluate the weather data. But I didn't. In my head, their retreat didn't apply to me. I was on a strict schedule; I had an 18:00 deadline at the Cosmostation, and my stubbornness blinded me to the shifting reality of the terrain. I dismissed their warning as "subjective difficulty."

**09:40** — Left the Ayusai area and pushed forward toward the upper valley. The trail began to steepen significantly. My pace slowed down to roughly 2.5 km/h due to the increasing incline and the weight of the rented, unadjusted gear. 

**11:15** — Reached the transition point near the approach to the Almaty-Alagir gorge. The temperature dropped noticeably to around -3°C, and the first patches of hard, old snow appeared on the rocks. My water supply was down by 30%.
